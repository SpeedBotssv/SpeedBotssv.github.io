SPEEDBOTS

The SpeedBot is designed to promote road safety through technology. Equipped with an advanced ultrasonic sensor, the vehicle is able to detect any object 20 cm away. Once it identifies an obstacle, the car brakes immediately, avoiding potential collisions. In addition to its automatic braking capability, the SpeedBot uses an intelligent decision-making system: after braking, it evaluates the available space around it, turning to the side that is free of obstructions. This capability is ideal for dense traffic environments, providing greater driving safety.

The SpeedBot also includes remote control functionality through a mobile app we have developed. With the app, the user can drive the car with precision, making it a useful tool for both safe driving practice and exploring its technology in controlled spaces. Its design combines a robust chassis and a servo motor that ensures efficient and smooth turns. This car is a practical and educational tool that, in addition to its road safety functionality, offers an interactive experience for those who wish to learn more about automated driving technologies.

MATERIALS

CHASsIS

Strong plastic chassis to ensure lightness and durability. I used additional support structures to secure the motors and other components firmly.

WHEELS

Sturdy wheels suitable for varied terrain, making sure the axles were strong enough to support the weight of the vehicle and sudden movements.

BLUETOOTH

Bluetooth module for control remote, ensuring stable and efficient communication within the desired operating area.

ARDUINO

Arduino Uno simplifies configuration and programming. This Arduino controls both the vehicle's movement and the auto-sensing system.

H BRIDGE

It reverses the polarity of the current that reaches the motor, which causes the motor to change direction. This is essential for a remote control car, as it allows the vehicle to move forward or backward as needed.

MOTORS

DC motors for propulsion, implementing an H bridge to control the direction of rotation. This allows them to move forward and backward using the L293D chip.

SERVOMOTOR

It receives the information sent from the pins, allowing it to give the order to the wheels to move in the direction and at the speed that is being requested.

ULTRASONIC SENSOR

PIR sensor for automatic detection of obstacles and other vehicles. We adjust the sensitivity parameters for fast and accurate responses during testing.

BATTERIES

Rechargeable batteries to power the Arduino and motors, correctly calculating the battery capacity to maintain the desired operating duration.
